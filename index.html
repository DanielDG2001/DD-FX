Macro Overview

Global FX markets open with a modestly risk-on tone, the USD softer across the board following mixed US data. The DXY is easing off recent highs as markets reassess Fed timing — rate cut expectations have been pushed further out, but the dollar is losing near-term momentum. EUR/USD is recovering toward the mid-1.07s, supported by a slightly less hawkish-than-feared ECB communication tone this week.

EUR / ISK

ISK is the story today. Yesterday's CBI decision to hike 25bps to 7.50% is providing a meaningful tailwind for the króna this morning. EUR/ISK has drifted lower overnight as the rate differential with the ECB (2.65%) widens to approximately 485bps — a level that continues to attract carry inflows. The pair looks offered into the 149.50–150.00 resistance zone. Watch for profit-taking on ISK longs if global risk sentiment turns.

USD / ISK

USD/ISK is seeing dual ISK strength — a softer dollar globally combined with the CBI hike is compressing the pair. The cross has fallen further than EUR/ISK in percentage terms as USD weakness amplifies the ISK bid. Near-term, ISK carry demand should keep USD/ISK capped below 140.00, though a reversal in US risk sentiment could quickly challenge that. Iceland's Q4 GDP contraction of –0.6% raises medium-term ISK fragility questions.

EUR / USD — G10 Reference

EUR/USD is recovering modestly, trading around the 1.0770–1.0820 band. ECB/Fed divergence remains the dominant narrative — the ECB is seen as nearly done, while the Fed retains scope to cut later in 2026. The pair faces heavy resistance at 1.0850. A sustained break above 1.09 would be required to shift the medium-term bias bullish.

Watch this afternoon: US jobless claims and Philly Fed index. A strong US print would reinvigorate dollar bulls and reverse this morning's ISK / USD / EUR moves. CBI follow-through communication also in focus — any explicit guidance on the rate path would directly move ISK crosses.

Iceland — CBI Context

The CBI's decision to raise rates to 7.50% signals the board views domestic inflation — running at 5.2% — as the primary concern, ahead of the Q4 GDP contraction. This is a stagflationary policy environment: tightening into weakness. For ISK, the hike is near-term supportive (carry, credibility) but the growth drag raises medium-term sustainability questions. The króna has strengthened over 6% against the dollar over the past year.
